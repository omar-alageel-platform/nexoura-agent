Research Brief · Round 0

B2B SaaS Pricing Page Patterns

For Stage 3 landing-page authoring. 6 sources, accessed 2026-05-21.

Key findings

  1. Flat-tier pricing dominates SMB-targeted B2B SaaS; 4 of 6 surveyed leaders lead with flat tiers above the fold [1][2][4][5].
  2. Usage-based pricing reads premium for developer/infrastructure tools but creates predictability anxiety in operator-buyer segments [1][6].
  3. Hybrid models (flat headline + usage overage line below) are the fastest-growing pattern in 2025–26 enterprise SaaS [6].
  4. "Contact us" enterprise tier consistently sits at the right of a 3- or 4-column grid; never as the only CTA [2][3][4].
  5. Feature-comparison tables average 18–24 rows; deeper tables correlate with longer time-to-decision in eye-tracking studies [6].

Recommended directions

Contrarian

Flat-tier-first

Best for: SMB-heavy ICP, predictable budgets.
Risk: leaves usage-volatile customers on the table.
Contrarian

Usage-based-first

Best for: dev-tools, infra, technical buyer.
Risk: operator-buyer reads "unpredictable bill".
Recommended

Hybrid headline

Best for: mixed ICP, operator persona with growth tail.
Risk: more copy to write, more PM cycles.

PM decision needed

Pick the page direction: Flat / Usage / Hybrid. Recommendation: Hybrid — operator-buyer persona is the dominant ICP for the engagement, and hybrid resolves the predictability tension without ceiling out growth accounts.
